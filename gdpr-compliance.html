GDPR Compliance Checklist

Data Protection Principles

  • Lawfulness, Fairness, and Transparency: Ensure processing is lawful, fair, and transparent. Provide clear privacy notices and establish a legal basis for processing.
  • Purpose Limitation: Collect data for specific, legitimate purposes and do not process it further in a way that is incompatible with those purposes.
  • Data Minimization: Collect only the data necessary for the intended purposes.
  • Accuracy: Keep personal data accurate and up to date.
  • Storage Limitation: Retain data only as long as necessary for the purpose for which it was collected.
  • Integrity and Confidentiality: Ensure data security through appropriate measures to protect against unauthorized access and accidental loss.

Rights of Data Subjects

  • Right to Access: Provide access to personal data upon request.
  • Right to Rectification: Allow data subjects to correct inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to Erasure: Enable data subjects to request deletion of their data under certain conditions.
  • Right to Restriction of Processing: Allow restriction of processing in specific situations.
  • Right to Data Portability: Provide data in a structured, commonly used, and machine-readable format.
  • Right to Object: Allow data subjects to object to data processing in certain circumstances.
  • Rights Related to Automated Decision-Making and Profiling: Ensure that individuals are not subject to automated decisions without their consent.

Data Protection Officer (DPO)

Appointment: Designate a Data Protection Officer if required and ensure they are properly resourced.

Responsibilities: The DPO should oversee data protection activities and advise on GDPR compliance.

Data Processing Agreements

Contracts: Ensure contracts with third-party processors include GDPR-compliant clauses.

Due Diligence: Conduct due diligence to ensure third-party processors comply with GDPR requirements.

Data Breach Management

Breach Notification: Implement procedures for detecting, reporting, and investigating data breaches.

Notification: Notify the supervisory authority within 72 hours and inform affected data subjects if necessary.

Records of Processing Activities

Documentation: Maintain detailed records of processing activities, including purposes and retention periods.

Review: Regularly review and update records to reflect changes in processing activities.

Training and Awareness

Training: Provide regular GDPR training for employees.

Awareness: Promote a culture of data protection within the organization.
